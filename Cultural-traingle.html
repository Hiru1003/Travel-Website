CULTURAL TRAINGLE

The Cultural Triangle is called after a triangle-shaped grouping of three significant historic sites.
Kandy is located in the southwest, Anuradhapura is in the north, and Polonaruwa is in the east. The triangle's other interesting locations are Mihintale, Ritigala, and Dambulla.

A treasure trove of ancient buildings, royal cities, and Buddhist temples may be found in Sri Lanka's fabled Cultural Triangle, which was once the center of early Sinhalese civilization. The three historical capitals of the island—Kandy, the first capital of the Sinhalese, Anuradhapura, and Polonnaruwa, the medieval capital—represent the triangle's three points.

This region, formerly home to ancient Sinhalese nobility, is rich in historic ruins and rebuilt temples and is a must-see when in Sri Lanka.
